Imagery Guidelines

The visual philosophy of the operational standard.

Our visual language is "The Operational Standard" — precision, purpose, quiet authority. We avoid aspirational or decorative imagery. What we show: real environments, active professionals, and the visible evidence of expertise at work.

Core Visual Focus

Photography captures professionals in clinical and operational settings: consultation rooms, training sessions, collaborative work, organised space. The mood is grounded, expert, and forward-moving.

Technical Execution

Controlled natural light or clean artificial lighting. No dramatic contrast or lifestyle warmth. Compositions feel observed, not staged. Shallow depth of field isolates the professional subject within a legible environment.

What to Avoid

Stock imagery with visible stock aesthetics. Clinical care imagery that positions Medi360 as a healthcare provider. Lifestyle or aspirational photography without a clear professional context.

Design Principles

The rules that hold the system together.

01

The No-Line Rule

1px solid borders for sectioning are prohibited. Content boundaries are defined through background colour shifts only. A card on a section background creates its own edge through tonal contrast, not a drawn line.

02

Tonal Layering

Depth is architectural, not decorative. Stack containers: white on surface-low, surface-low on surface-high. The shift in surface tier creates hierarchy. Heavy drop shadows are never used.

03

Intentional Asymmetry

Break rigid grids with offset typography and overlapping elements. A headline that bleeds into the left margin reads as editorial authority, not disorder. Dynamic layouts suggest a forward-thinking consultancy.

04

The Neon Green Laser

Active Pulse (#C0F968) is used sparingly as a direction signal, not a background. It points the user's eye toward a single conversion action per screen. When overused, it loses all power.

Do

Use #C0F968 sparingly as a focal point toward conversion actions

Allow asymmetrical margins. Let headlines read as editorial signatures

Use surface-bright (#F7FAFD) for primary content areas

Left-align all long-form body text

Don't

Use pure black for text. Use #181C1E to maintain the tonal range

Use standard box shadows. If it looks like a lifted card, it is too heavy

Use dividers between list items. Increase spacing or shift surface tier instead

Centre-align long-form body copy
